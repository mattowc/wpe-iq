How The Brain Works

Learning occurs in 2 phases:

  1. Sensory Input
  2. Neurological Processing

The first phase, sensory input, refers to all the information gathered by our senses: sight, sound, touch, smell, and taste. Schools and tutors try to help kids learn by providing sensory data loaded with information. They will use sight in the form of notes and diagrams; they will use sound in the form of lectures and explanations; they may even use touch through demonstrations that call for class participation.

In order for this sensory data to make sense, our brains must process it. Our brain processes information by sending sensory data along neurological connections to the appropriate lobe. When these connections are not properly developed, the affected person exhibits a learning disorder. Schools do not address the processing aspect of learning.
